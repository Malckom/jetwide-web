Package Overview

Discover Kenya's coastal heritage on this enriching educational journey to Mombasa. Students will explore centuries of Swahili history at Fort Jesus, witness remarkable conservation efforts at Haller Park, and discover marine ecosystems at Mombasa Marine National Park. This trip blends history, conservation, and marine biology into an unforgettable coastal learning experience.

📍 Destination: Mombasa, Kenya Coast
🎓 Educational Focus: Kenyan History, Conservation, Marine Biology, Swahili Culture

Trip Highlights

  • Fort Jesus UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • Haller Park conservation and nature reserve
  • Mombasa Old Town - Swahili architecture and culture
  • Mombasa Marine National Park - glass-bottom boat ride
  • Giraffe feeding and wildlife encounters
  • Butterfly pavilion and crocodile farm
  • Spice markets and traditional crafts

Educational Value

  • Kenyan coastal history - Portuguese, Omani, and British influences

Why Mombasa?

Mombasa offers students a unique window into Kenya's rich coastal heritage, Islamic influence, and Swahili culture. The trip provides hands-on learning about marine conservation, historical significance, and environmental restoration—all within Kenya's borders, making it accessible and affordable for schools.

Detailed Itinerary

Day One: Departure and Arrival in Mombasa

Meals: Lunch, Dinner | Overnight: Mombasa Beach Hotel

The journey begins with departure from Nairobi, either by SGR train or by school bus. Along the way, students will enjoy the scenic landscapes of Tsavo, which provide an opportunity to observe Kenya's diverse geography and wildlife from the train/bus windows.

Upon arrival in Mombasa, the group will check into the hotel, enjoy lunch, and have some time to rest. In the evening, there will be an orientation session, safety briefing, and group bonding activities. This day helps students transition smoothly into the trip, setting the tone for both learning and teamwork.

Day Two: Exploration of Haller Park

Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner | Overnight: Mombasa Beach Hotel

The second day will be dedicated to Haller Park, a famous conservation area that was once an abandoned quarry but has since been transformed into a thriving nature reserve. Students will experience:

  • Giraffe feeding - Hands-on interaction with these gentle giants
  • Guided nature walks - Learning about various indigenous plants and trees
  • Butterfly pavilion - Observing the life cycle of butterflies
  • Wildlife encounters - Hippos, crocodiles, and giant tortoises
  • Environmental education session - Ecosystem restoration and conservation

This visit teaches the importance of environmental responsibility and helps children understand how human actions can positively transform the environment. Students learn about habitat restoration, biodiversity, and sustainable practices.

Day Three: Fort Jesus, Old Town, and Mombasa Marine National Park

Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner | Overnight: Mombasa Beach Hotel

The third day begins with a cultural and historical immersion at Fort Jesus, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Students will:

  • Explore the 16th-century Portuguese fortress
  • Learn about the Portuguese, Omani, and British influences on Kenya's coast
  • Discover how Mombasa played a key role in the history of Indian Ocean trade
  • View historical artifacts and architecture

Afterward, they will take a short-guided walk through Mombasa Old Town, experiencing:

  • Narrow streets with authentic Swahili architecture
  • Intricately carved doors and balconies
  • Vibrant spice markets and local crafts
  • Living Swahili culture and Islamic heritage

This morning session offers a deeper appreciation of Kenya's coastal identity, blending African, Arab, and European heritage.

In the afternoon, the group will proceed to Mombasa Marine National Park and Reserve, where they can enjoy a glass-bottom boat ride to view:

  • Colorful coral reefs and marine life
  • Tropical fish species
  • Sea turtles and starfish
  • Marine ecosystem interactions

The experience allows students to witness firsthand the importance of marine conservation and understand the fragile balance of aquatic ecosystems. This combination of history, culture, and marine exploration makes Day Three a highlight of the trip, expanding the students' knowledge from land to sea.

Day Four: Departure to Nairobi

Meals: Breakfast | Overnight: N/A

On the final day, students will have breakfast, check out, and begin their journey back to Nairobi. The return trip provides an opportunity for reflection and discussion about the lessons learned:

  • Conservation efforts at Haller Park
  • Historical significance of Fort Jesus
  • Swahili culture and coastal heritage
  • Marine ecosystems and coral reef protection

By the end of the trip, the children will not only have enjoyed themselves but will also have gained valuable educational insights into Kenya's heritage, environment, and culture.
