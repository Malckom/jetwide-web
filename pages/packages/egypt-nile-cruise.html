7-Day Egypt Nile Cruise: Aswan to Luxor

PRICE On Request per person
DURATION 7 Days / 6 Nights
TOUR TYPE Cultural Cruise
DESTINATIONS Aswan • Kom Ombo • Edfu • Luxor

Cruise Overview

Embark on an unforgettable 7-day journey through the heart of ancient Egypt aboard a luxurious Nile cruise. Sail from Aswan to Luxor, following the legendary river that gave birth to one of the world's greatest civilizations.

This cultural voyage takes you through thousands of years of history, with guided shore excursions to magnificent temples, tombs, and monuments that have stood the test of time. Experience the grandeur of Pharaonic Egypt while enjoying modern comfort and hospitality on board your floating hotel.

Cruise Highlights

  • Visit the magnificent Temple of Philae, dedicated to the goddess Isis, on Agilkia Island
  • Explore the double Temple of Kom Ombo, uniquely dedicated to two gods: Sobek and Horus
  • Marvel at the incredibly well-preserved Temple of Horus at Edfu
  • Discover the Valley of the Kings with its 63 royal tombs
  • Visit the spectacular Temple of Queen Hatshepsut at Deir el-Bahari
  • Stand in awe at the Colossi of Memnon, two massive stone statues
  • Explore the vast Karnak Temple Complex, the largest religious building ever constructed
  • Experience the enchanting Luxor Temple illuminated at night

Important Notes

  • Climate: Egypt is hot and dry. Temperatures can exceed 40°C in summer. Winter (Nov-Feb) offers pleasant weather.
  • Dress Code: Modest clothing recommended for temple visits. Light, breathable fabrics ideal for the heat.
  • Photography: Camera fees apply at most sites. Video cameras have separate charges.
  • Visa: E-visa available online or visa on arrival for most nationalities (USD 25).
  • Currency: Egyptian Pound (EGP). US Dollars and Euros widely accepted at tourist sites.
  • Tipping: Tipping (baksheesh) is customary. Budget USD 5-10 per day for guides and crew.
  • Health: Drink only bottled water. Bring sunscreen, hat, and any prescription medications.
  • Best Time: October to April offers ideal weather. Summer (May-Sep) is very hot but less crowded.

Detailed Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day 1

Arrival in Aswan - Embarkation

Upon arrival at Aswan Airport or railway station, you will be met and transferred to your Nile cruise ship. Complete embarkation procedures and settle into your cabin.

After lunch on board, begin your Egyptian adventure with a visit to the High Dam, an engineering marvel completed in 1970 that created Lake Nasser, one of the world's largest artificial lakes.

Continue to the Temple of Philae, dedicated to the goddess Isis. This beautiful temple was relocated to Agilkia Island as part of the UNESCO Nubia Campaign. Take a short motorboat ride to reach this enchanting island sanctuary.

Return to the ship for dinner and your first overnight on the Nile.

Accommodation: Nile Cruise (Full Board)

Meals: Lunch & Dinner

Highlights: Aswan High Dam, Temple of Philae, Agilkia Island

Day 2

Kom Ombo Temple

Enjoy breakfast as the cruise sails north toward Kom Ombo. Watch traditional felucca sailboats and riverside villages pass by from the sun deck.

Mid-morning, the ship docks at Kom Ombo. Visit the unique Temple of Kom Ombo, which stands directly on the Nile's edge. This temple is unusual as it's dedicated to two gods: Sobek (the crocodile god) and Horus (the falcon-headed god). The temple's perfect symmetry reflects this dual dedication.

Explore the fascinating Crocodile Museum, which houses mummified crocodiles discovered in the area. Learn about ancient Egyptian medical instruments depicted in the temple's reliefs.

Return to the ship for lunch as it continues sailing to Edfu. Afternoon at leisure to relax on deck or enjoy the ship's facilities.

Accommodation: Nile Cruise (Full Board)

Meals: Breakfast, Lunch & Dinner

Highlights: Temple of Kom Ombo, Crocodile Museum, scenic Nile sailing

Day 3

Edfu Temple & Sailing to Luxor

After breakfast, enjoy a unique experience as you take a traditional horse-drawn carriage ride through Edfu town to reach the temple (approximately 15 minutes).

Visit the Temple of Horus at Edfu, the best-preserved temple in Egypt. Built between 237 and 57 BC during the Ptolemaic period, this magnificent structure is dedicated to Horus, the falcon god. Marvel at the massive pylons, detailed hieroglyphics, and the imposing statue of Horus as a falcon.

Return to the ship via horse-drawn carriage and enjoy lunch as the cruise continues sailing toward Luxor through the Esna Lock. This is a fascinating engineering process to watch as the ship is raised or lowered to navigate different water levels.

Afternoon at leisure on board. Perhaps enjoy afternoon tea on the sun deck while watching the lush Nile Valley landscape pass by.

Arrive in Luxor in the evening. Optional visit to Luxor Temple beautifully illuminated at night (additional cost).

Accommodation: Nile Cruise (Full Board)

Meals: Breakfast, Lunch & Dinner

Highlights: Temple of Horus, horse-carriage ride, Esna Lock, illuminated Luxor Temple

Day 4

West Bank of Luxor

Early breakfast followed by an extensive tour of Luxor's West Bank, the ancient Theban Necropolis.

Begin at the Valley of the Kings, the burial ground of New Kingdom pharaohs (1550-1070 BC). Your entrance ticket includes visits to three tombs (note: King Tutankhamun's tomb requires a separate ticket). Descend into these underground chambers adorned with colorful hieroglyphics and scenes from ancient Egyptian religious texts.

Continue to the impressive Temple of Queen Hatshepsut at Deir el-Bahari. This unique temple is built into the cliff face in a series of terraces. Learn about Egypt's most successful female pharaoh who ruled for over 20 years.

Stop briefly at the Colossi of Memnon, two massive seated statues of Pharaoh Amenhotep III that have stood guard for over 3,400 years.

Return to the ship for lunch. Afternoon free to rest or explore Luxor town independently.

Accommodation: Nile Cruise (Full Board)

Meals: Breakfast, Lunch & Dinner

Highlights: Valley of the Kings (3 tombs), Temple of Hatshepsut, Colossi of Memnon

Day 5

East Bank of Luxor - Karnak & Luxor Temples

After breakfast, disembark for a tour of Luxor's East Bank monuments.

Visit the awe-inspiring Karnak Temple Complex, the largest religious building ever constructed. This vast open-air museum covers about 200 acres and was built over 2,000 years by successive pharaohs. Walk through the Great Hypostyle Hall with its 134 massive columns, each 69 feet tall. Marvel at the sacred lake, obelisks, and countless statues and hieroglyphics.

Continue to Luxor Temple, connected to Karnak by the ancient Avenue of Sphinxes (currently being restored). This temple, built primarily by Amenhotep III and Ramesses II, served as a setting for religious festivals. Admire the massive first pylon, the granite obelisk (its twin is in Paris), and the colonnade of papyrus-bud columns.

Return to the ship for lunch. Afternoon and evening at leisure. Perhaps enjoy one last swim in the sun deck pool or shop for souvenirs in Luxor's bazaar.

Accommodation: Nile Cruise (Full Board)

Meals: Breakfast, Lunch & Dinner

Highlights: Karnak Temple Complex, Luxor Temple, Avenue of Sphinxes

Day 6

Luxor - Optional Excursions

Full day at leisure on board the cruise. This is your opportunity to relax, revisit any sites independently, or join optional excursions (additional cost).

Optional Activities (not included):

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride over Luxor's West Bank - Experience sunrise views of the temples and Nile Valley from above (approximately USD 100-150)
  • Valley of the Queens - Visit the tombs of ancient Egyptian queens and royal children
  • Deir el-Medina - Explore the village of ancient tomb workers with beautifully decorated tombs
  • Luxor Museum - See an excellent collection of artifacts from the Theban temples and necropolis

Alternatively, simply relax on board, enjoy the pool, and take in your final evening on the Nile. Tonight might feature a special Egyptian-themed dinner or entertainment.

Accommodation: Nile Cruise (Full Board)

Meals: Breakfast, Lunch & Dinner

Day 7

Disembarkation - Departure

After breakfast, complete check-out procedures and disembark from the cruise.

Transfer to Luxor Airport or railway station for your onward journey. If your flight is later in the day, luggage storage can usually be arranged while you explore Luxor town for last-minute shopping.

Depart with unforgettable memories of ancient Egypt and the timeless beauty of the Nile.

Meals: Breakfast

Note: If you wish to extend your stay in Egypt, we can arrange additional nights in Cairo to visit the Pyramids of Giza, Egyptian Museum, and Khan el-Khalili bazaar.
