Detailed Itinerary

Day 01: Arrival in Cairo

Meals: None | Overnight: Cairo Hotel

Arrive at Cairo International Airport where our representative will be waiting to welcome and assist you. You'll be transferred in comfort to your hotel for check-in. Enjoy a peaceful evening and prepare for the adventure ahead. Evening orientation session about the historical journey you're about to embark on.

Day 02: Cairo Sightseeing

Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner | Overnight: Cairo Hotel

After breakfast, begin your full-day tour with a visit to the Pyramids of Giza—Cheops, Chephren, and Menkaure—some of the most iconic monuments in the world. Admire the Great Pyramid, the only remaining wonder of the ancient world, and marvel at the Sphinx, standing guard over the plateau.

Students will learn about:

  • Ancient Egyptian engineering and construction techniques
  • The purpose and significance of pyramid construction
  • The symbolism of the Sphinx
  • Daily life in ancient Egypt

Continue to the Egyptian Museum, home to an extensive collection of ancient treasures including the golden mask of King Tutankhamun and relics spanning millennia. Return to your hotel for an overnight in Cairo.

Day 03: Fly to Aswan / Sightseeing / Nile Cruise Embarkation

Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner | Overnight: Nile Cruise

After breakfast, transfer to Cairo Airport for your flight to Aswan. Upon arrival, explore:

  • Aswan High Dam - A 20th-century engineering feat that controls the flow of the Nile. Students learn about modern engineering and water management.
  • Unfinished Obelisk - Located in ancient granite quarries, see the massive obelisk still embedded in rock, providing insights into ancient stone-working techniques.

Board your Nile Cruise for check-in and lunch onboard. In the afternoon, take a motorboat to Philae Island to visit the beautiful Philae Temple, dedicated to the goddess Isis. This temple was relocated stone by stone to save it from flooding—a remarkable feat of modern archaeology.

Enjoy dinner and overnight onboard in Aswan, beginning your 3-night cruise journey.

Day 04: Kom Ombo & Edfu Temples

Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner | Overnight: Nile Cruise

Sail toward Kom Ombo and visit the unique double temple dedicated to Sobek, the crocodile god, and Horus the Elder. This is the only temple in Egypt dedicated to two gods. Admire the hieroglyphics depicting ancient medical instruments and visit the attached crocodile museum with mummified crocodiles.

Return to the cruise for lunch while sailing to Edfu. At Edfu, explore the Temple of Horus, one of the best-preserved temples in Egypt, known for its towering pylons and detailed carvings. Students will:

  • Study hieroglyphic inscriptions
  • Learn about ancient Egyptian religion and mythology
  • Understand temple architecture and layout
  • Explore the symbolism in wall carvings

Sail onward toward Luxor. Overnight onboard.

Day 05: East Bank of Luxor

Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner | Overnight: Nile Cruise

After breakfast, visit the magnificent Karnak Temple Complex, the largest religious structure ever built. Walk through the awe-inspiring Hypostyle Hall with its 134 giant papyrus-shaped columns, some reaching 21 meters high. This vast complex was expanded over 2,000 years by successive pharaohs.

Then continue to Luxor Temple, constructed by Amenhotep III and expanded by Ramses II. This temple was used for religious festivals and royal ceremonies and is beautifully illuminated at night. Students explore:

  • The Avenue of Sphinxes connecting Karnak and Luxor temples
  • Ancient Egyptian religious ceremonies
  • The evolution of temple architecture
  • Royal propaganda and power displays

Return to the cruise for dinner and overnight.

Day 06: Luxor West Bank / Fly back to Cairo

Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner | Overnight: Cairo Hotel

Disembark after breakfast and visit the West Bank of Luxor—the ancient necropolis of Thebes. Start with the Colossi of Memnon—two towering statues of Amenhotep III standing 18 meters tall.

Then explore three tombs in the Valley of the Kings, where Egypt's pharaohs were laid to rest in chambers richly decorated with scenes of the afterlife. Students will:

  • Discover the Book of the Dead illustrations
  • Learn about mummification processes
  • Understand ancient Egyptian beliefs about the afterlife
  • See the vivid colors preserved for 3,000+ years

Conclude with a visit to the majestic Temple of Queen Hatshepsut, rising in terraces against the cliffs of Deir el-Bahari. This unique architectural masterpiece honors Egypt's most successful female pharaoh.

Transfer to Luxor Airport for your flight to Cairo. Upon arrival, transfer to your hotel. Overnight in Cairo.

Day 07: Departure

Meals: Breakfast | Overnight: N/A

After breakfast, check out from your hotel and transfer to Cairo International Airport for your onward flight. Students depart with comprehensive knowledge of ancient Egyptian civilization, having experienced one of history's greatest cultures firsthand. We hope your journey through the wonders of Egypt leaves lasting memories and valuable educational insights.
